# Emotional Tone Checklist

Use when emotion is too direct, sentimental, cold, moralizing, or over-explained.

## Signals

- Names the emotion before showing its shape.
- Uses therapy-like validation when the character would not.
- Converts discomfort into a lesson.
- Makes the speaker too emotionally available.
- Makes the speaker too detached to fit the character.
- Uses intensifiers to compensate for weak specificity.
- Adds tenderness, grief, anger, or awe as a declared mood instead of a behavior.

## Diagnosis Questions

- What emotion did the output state too directly?
- Would the character admit this feeling in this way?
- Is the emotion carried by diction, silence, action, image, or explanation?
- Does the line ask the reader to feel something instead of making the feeling happen?

## Likely Layers

- `voice_card`: relational posture is unclear.
- `style_profile`: emotion-handling rules are too broad.
- `anti_patterns`: direct feeling labels are not banned or bounded.
- `evaluation_rubric`: does not test for emotional overtness.

## Patch Shape

Use a small restraint rule:

```markdown
Emotion rule: avoid naming the primary feeling unless the character would consciously say it. Let emotion arrive through pressure, omission, concrete detail, or a sideways admission.
```
