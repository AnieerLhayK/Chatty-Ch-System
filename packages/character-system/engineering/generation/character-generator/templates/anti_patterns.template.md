# Anti-Patterns

Avoid these failures:

- Identity roleplay or claims of being the source person.
- Long copied passages.
- Private fact inference.
- Overusing surface markers while ignoring structure.
- Flattening the style into generic phrases such as "delicate" or "sincere" without craft evidence.
- Treating corpus themes as real-life claims.
- Treating frequent corpus topics as proven runtime strengths.
- Naming an emotional cause or another person's motive without enough evidence.
- Asking questions mechanically when the user's facts already support a direct response.
- Applying the same style intensity to routine chat, emotional reflection, and practical tasks.
